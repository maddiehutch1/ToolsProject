# Crisis Resources

## Important Notice
If you or someone you know is in immediate danger, call **911** (or your local emergency number) immediately.

The resources below are available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, and are free and confidential.

---

## 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline
The 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline is the primary crisis resource in the United States, operated by SAMHSA.

- **Call or text:** 988
- **Chat:** [988lifeline.org](https://988lifeline.org)
- **Available:** 24/7, free, confidential
- **Who it serves:** Anyone experiencing suicidal thoughts, mental health crisis, substance use crisis, or emotional distress — and people who are concerned about a loved one
- **Specialized lines:** Veterans Crisis Line (press 1), LGBTQ+ line (press 3), Spanish-language line (press 2)

When you call or text 988, you are connected to a trained counselor at a local crisis center. The counselor listens, provides support, and can connect you to local resources if needed.

---

## Crisis Text Line
- **Text:** HOME to **741741**
- **Available:** 24/7, free, confidential
- **Who it serves:** Anyone in crisis — particularly useful for those who prefer text communication, are in a location where a phone call isn't possible, or feel more comfortable with text
- **How it works:** Text HOME to 741741. You'll be connected to a trained Crisis Counselor within minutes. The conversation is confidential. Counselors are trained volunteers supervised by licensed mental health professionals.

---

## Veterans Crisis Line
- **Call:** 988, then press 1
- **Text:** 838255
- **Chat:** [veteranscrisisline.net](https://www.veteranscrisisline.net)
- **Who it serves:** Veterans, service members, National Guard and Reserve members, and their families and friends
- **Available:** 24/7, staffed by responders who are many Veterans themselves

---

## NAMI Helpline (National Alliance on Mental Illness)
- **Call:** 1-800-950-NAMI (6264)
- **Text:** "NAMI" to 741741
- **Email:** helpline@nami.org
- **Available:** Monday–Friday, 10 AM–10 PM ET
- **Who it serves:** Individuals with mental illness, family members, and caregivers seeking information, referrals, and support
- **What it offers:** Information on mental health conditions and treatment options, local referrals for mental health services, support navigating the mental health care system

NAMI also offers free peer support programs including NAMI Family Support Group and NAMI Connection Recovery Support Group (in-person and online).

---

## SAMHSA National Helpline
SAMHSA (Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration) provides a free treatment referral and information service.

- **Call:** 1-800-662-HELP (4357)
- **TTY:** 1-800-487-4889
- **Website:** [findtreatment.gov](https://findtreatment.gov)
- **Available:** 24/7, 365 days a year, free, confidential
- **Languages:** English and Spanish
- **Who it serves:** Individuals and families facing mental health or substance use disorders
- **What it offers:** Information about mental health and substance use disorders, local treatment facility and support group referrals, does not provide counseling

## Crisis Safety Planning
If you are working with a therapist or have experienced recurring crises, a safety plan is one of the most effective evidence-based tools for managing suicidal thoughts.

A safety plan typically includes:
1. **Warning signs** — thoughts, feelings, or behaviors that signal a crisis may be building
2. **Internal coping strategies** — things you can do alone to manage distress (grounding techniques, distraction)
3. **Social contacts for distraction** — people you can contact for support (not necessarily to discuss the crisis)
4. **People to ask for help** — specific trusted people who know about your mental health
5. **Professional contacts** — therapist name and number, psychiatrist, crisis line
6. **Making the environment safer** — steps to reduce access to means

The Stanley-Brown Safety Planning Intervention is available free at [sprc.org](https://sprc.org).

## When to Call 911
Call 911 immediately if:
- Someone has already taken steps toward suicide (e.g., taken pills, has a weapon)
- There is immediate physical danger to self or others
- The person is unconscious or unresponsive
- You believe waiting for other resources would result in harm

**You will not get someone "in trouble" by calling for help. Calling may save their life.**
